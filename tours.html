Ross Castle

Ross Castle is a picturesque 15th-century tower house located on the edge of Lough Leane in Killarney National Park, County Kerry, Ireland. Originally built by the O'Donoghue clan, the castle has a rich history and is notable for its well-preserved condition and beautiful lakeside setting. It played a significant role during the Irish Confederate Wars in the 17th century and is associated with legends involving the O'Donoghue chieftains.

Kylemore Abbey & Victorian Walled Garden

Kylemore Abbey & Victorian Walled Garden is a magnificent estate located in Connemara, County Galway, Ireland. Originally built as a private home in 1867 by Mitchell Henry for his wife Margaret, the estate features stunning architecture inspired by the Gothic Revival style.
Nestled at the base of Druchruach Mountain and beside Kylemore Lough, the setting is famously picturesque.
